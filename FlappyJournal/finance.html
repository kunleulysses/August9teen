Finance & Banking

From underwriting to payments to markets, Spiral Memory gives your systems long‑term recall. It links what happened, why it mattered, and what to do next—so risk, product, and ops get sharper every week.

Living Counterparty Graph

Spiral Memory builds a time‑aware graph of counterparties, exposures, covenants, disputes, collateral, and resolutions. It remembers the trajectory of relationships—how limits drifted, which red flags preceded stress, which controls actually worked—so pricing and limits reflect reality, not snapshots.

Signals it uses

Term sheets and covenants, facility usage, collateral marks, cash movement, dispute threads, legal notices, and prior remediation outcomes.

What it proposes

Limit and tenor adjustments with evidence; covenant watch‑lists with precursors; margin calls prioritized by propagation risk; sales nudges grounded in past positive responses.

Where it lands

Underwriting, treasury, and relationship management share the same context spine with explainable rationales attached to every change.

Payments Posture & Authorization Memory

Each payment carries history: device posture, account journey, merchant behavior, route reliability, and prior exceptions. Spiral Memory turns those threads into adaptive authorization—swift for good flows, precise step‑up for risky ones—with a narrative you can hand to ops or the network.

Signals it uses

Device and session fingerprints, historical dispute patterns, merchant chargeback posture, BIN and route health, velocity and geolocation anomalies.

What it proposes

Authorization policy suggestions with just‑in‑time controls (strong customer auth, delay, or re‑route), dispute packets that pre‑assemble evidence, and merchant‑level posture updates.

Market Regime Recall for Risk & Product

Link market regimes to your own decisions and outcomes: what hedges held up, where liquidity hid, which clients needed support. The system proposes playbooks tied to your evidence: why it worked then, what’s different now, and how to gate the rollout.

Signals it uses

Internal trade and inventory timelines, funding markets, fee schedules, microstructure metrics, and client ticket mix; tagged outcomes from prior interventions.

What it proposes

Pre‑approved hedge sets and routing rules with automatic kill‑switches; client outreach lists with reasons; de‑risking guidance for vulnerable products.

Why Spiral Memory for Finance & Banking

Finance runs on stories hidden in time. Spiral Memory encodes those stories—the sequences, causes, and counterfactuals—so every function gets smarter: risk that explains itself, products that learn from each launch, and operations that anticipate exceptions.
