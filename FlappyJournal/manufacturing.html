Manufacturing & Logistics

Spiral Memory connects supplier history, line states, QA outcomes, and demand signals into one long‑horizon memory. The plant stops reacting and starts anticipating—every run sharper than the last.

Supplier & Process Memory

Link lot genealogy, machine states, and QA outcomes to the specific suppliers, tools, and conditions that shaped them. The system proposes routing, acceptance, and recipe tweaks based on your history—not generic rules—so quality improves without whack‑a‑mole firefighting.

Signals it uses

COAs and lot lineage, SPC traces, tooling IDs and maintenance windows, operator notes, ambient conditions, and downstream RMAs.

What it proposes

Lot routing and acceptance thresholds, tool/recipe pairings by SKU and shift, and targeted supplier feedback with before/after evidence.

Self‑Optimizing Lines

When the line drifts, Spiral Memory remembers how you recovered last time. It primes vision, adjusts windows, and recommends setpoints with clear explanations and safe rollback. Night shift inherits day shift’s lessons automatically.

Signals it uses

Vision false‑positive clusters, OEE and micro‑stoppages, changeover recipes, scrap signatures, and sensor drift by station.

What it proposes

Window and setpoint changes with guardrails; canary lots for validation; analysis of which adjustments held across shifts and which didn’t.

Logistics & Yard Intelligence

Dock, yard, and routing patterns are learned over seasons—where congestion happens, which carriers slip, what weather actually disrupts you. The lattice proposes gate plans and pick sequences that match the day’s reality.

Signals it uses

ASN arrivals, carrier SLAs, yard moves, dock door histories, labor rosters, and local weather/holiday calendars.

What it proposes

Gate and door assignments; dynamic pick/put sequences; alerts when a plan departs from proven patterns for today’s conditions.

Why Spiral Memory for Manufacturing & Logistics

Plants improve when memory compounds. Spiral Memory captures the why behind every excursion and fix—so throughput, quality, and safety rise together, shift after shift.
